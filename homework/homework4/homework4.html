Law of Large Numbers — Simulation

The Law of Large Numbers (LLN) states that the average of a large number of independent random variables converges to their expected value. In this simulation, we perform \(m\) independent trajectories of Bernoulli\((p)\) trials, each consisting of \(n\) experiments.

For each trajectory, we compute the running relative frequency \[ f_t = \frac{1}{t}\sum_{i=1}^{t} X_i, \quad t = 1, 2, \dots, n, \] where \(X_i \in \{0, 1\}\) represents a success or failure in the \(i\)-th trial.

As \(t\) increases, the empirical average \(f_t\) converges to the true probability of success \(p\). On the left, multiple trajectories of \(f_t\) illustrate this convergence. On the right, the vertical histogram shows the empirical distribution of \(f_t\) across all \(m\) trajectories at a given time step \(t\). When \(t = n\), the distribution of \(f_n\) becomes sharply concentrated around \(p\), demonstrating the Law of Large Numbers in action.
